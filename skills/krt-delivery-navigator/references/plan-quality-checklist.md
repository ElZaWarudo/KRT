# Plan Quality Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a delivery plan or deciding whether a planning artifact is credible enough to hand off.

## Source Integrity

- Is the plan visibly based on a requirements source?
- Are assumptions distinguished from confirmed requirements?
- Is scope creep avoided?

## Structure And Traceability

- Does each major task, workstream, or backlog slice trace back to a requirement, constraint, dependency, or risk?
- Are in-scope and out-of-scope boundaries visible?
- Are partial deliveries or milestone outcomes explicit?

## Prioritization Quality

- Is ordering explained by value, dependency, risk, or learning?
- Are critical path items visible?
- Does the plan avoid low-value busywork ahead of risk-reducing work?

## Schedule Quality

- Are estimates or dates presented with an honest level of confidence?
- Is sequencing realistic for the stated dependencies?
- Are milestones tied to deliverables rather than vague activity?

## Ownership Quality

- Does each major area have a visible owner, team, or responsible role?
- Are approvals or stakeholder checkpoints clear?

## Risk Quality

- Are the main technical, schedule, scope, or dependency risks visible?
- Does each important risk have a mitigation, contingency, or monitoring action?

## Common Smells

Challenge plans that show these symptoms:

- tasks appear without linkage to requirements
- backlog is just a dump of activities with no ordering logic
- milestones describe meetings instead of outcomes
- schedule precision is higher than evidence supports
- technology choices appear without justification or requirement pressure
- risks are listed but not acted upon
- team names are omitted when capacity or ownership matters
- "testing" appears only at the end instead of being integrated into delivery slices

## Ready-To-Hand-Off Gate

Treat the plan as handoff-ready only when most answers are yes:

- Could a team start execution without inventing the project scope?
- Is the first delivery slice clear enough to begin?
- Are the main dependencies and risks visible?
- Is the chosen planning shape appropriate for the uncertainty level?
- Would a stakeholder understand what will be delivered first and why?
